# CHUNK 005 — ACADEMIC STRUCTURE: COURSES, CREDITS, AND COURSE TYPES

**Academic Year:** Two semesters per year plus a summer term (when offered). The semester system involves modular courses, continuous and internal evaluation, and letter grading.

**Credits (Units):** Each course carries a credit value (called "units"). A standard course is typically 3 units; 4-unit courses include a practical component.
- Minimum units per semester: 9
- Maximum units per semester: 25
- No exceptions are made to this range.

**Lecture (L), Practical (P), Units (U):** Listed in timetable as L-P-U. Eg: 3-0-3 (3 lectures, no practical, 3 units) or 3-1-4 (3 lectures, 1 practical, 4 units).

**Course Types:**
1. **Standard Courses:** Lecture + Tutorial + (sometimes) Practical.
2. **Labs:** Typically 1-unit, solely practical/experiment-based. No theory component. Always evaluated.
3. **Formal Projects:** 3-credit courses evaluated on a semester-long project under a professor.

**Course Categories:**
- **CDCs (Core Disciplinary Courses):** Mandatory courses for your branch. Cannot be dropped. Must obtain a valid grade to graduate.
- **DEIs / DELs (Disciplinary Electives):** Courses aligned to your branch. Must complete minimum 4 DEIs or 12 credits to graduate.
- **HuELs (Humanities Electives):** Diverse courses — social studies, literature, philosophy, cinema, social theory. Must complete minimum 3 HuELs or 8 credits to graduate.
- **OpELs (Open Electives):** Open to anyone meeting prerequisites. Explore subjects outside core curriculum; can be used for a minor. Must complete minimum 5 OpELs or 15 credits to graduate.

**Prerequisites:** Some courses require prior completion of specified courses. Check the Timetable or Academic Bulletin carefully.

**Minor Degree:** By paying a fee (~Rs. 28,000 for 2023-24 batch, payable in two installments of Rs. 14,000 each), meeting the CGPA cutoff, and completing required courses, students can earn an additional degree. Start planning from second year.

# CHUNK 006 — GRADING SYSTEM AND CGPA

**Letter Grades and Grade Points (10-point scale):**
- A = 10
- A- = 9
- B = 8
- B- = 7 (average mark for most courses)
- C = 6
- C- = 5
- D = 4
- E = 2 (Fail — but credit counts toward average)
- NC (Not Cleared) — not a grade; means teacher could not evaluate the student
- RC — Repeat Course required

**SGPA (Semester Grade Point Average):** Weighted average of grade points in a semester, divided by total credits that semester.

**CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average):** Weighted average of grade points across ALL letter grades received since entry. Expressed on 10-point scale. A student is deemed to have cleared a course if they obtain any letter grade (A to E).

**Degree Classification (Integrated First Degree):**
- Distinction: CGPA ≥ 8.5 (approximately)
- First Division: CGPA ≥ 6.5 (approximately)
- Second Division: CGPA ≥ 5.0 (approximately)

Note: Higher Degree and Doctoral programmes do not receive division classifications.

**NC (Not Cleared) Report:** If a student fails to cooperate with the teacher to such an extent that the teacher cannot award any grade, the teacher gives an NC report. NC is NOT a grade — student has not cleared the course.

**E Grade:** A failing grade (2 points). Students are allowed at most one E grade per semester in integrated First Degree programmes. Getting more than one E triggers an Academic Counselling Cell (ACC) meeting.

# CHUNK 007 — MINIMUM ACADEMIC REQUIREMENTS

Students must meet these requirements throughout their stay:

1. **CGPA ≥ 4.5** at the end of every semester (for Integrated First Degree students).
   - CGPA ≥ 5.5 for Higher Degree / Ph.D. students.
2. **Not more than one E grade in a semester** (Integrated First Degree). Higher Degree: no E grades allowed.
3. **Pace of progress:** At any stage, a student must not have spent more than 50% extra time than prescribed for their programme up to that stage.

**Consequences of not meeting requirements:**
- Students are placed under the **Academic Counselling Board (ACB)** (appointed by the Senate).
- ACB monitors and guides students to rehabilitate academically.
- ACB has authority to take action including **discontinuance** of the student or **transfer to another programme**.
- For Ph.D.: handled by **Departmental Research Committee (DRC)** and **Doctoral Counselling Committee**.

# CHUNK 008 — MAKE-UP TESTS, WITHDRAWALS, AND ACADEMIC FLEXIBILITY

**Make-up Test (Makeup):** If a student misses an evaluative component (quiz, lab, etc.) due to medical reasons, they may apply for a make-up. To claim a make-up, present a Medical Centre receipt as proof.
- General advice: Apply for make-ups only for labs. Make-up papers are harder than regular papers and approval is not guaranteed.
- Withdrawal requests via email or telephone are not entertained; must be done in person/officially.

**Withdrawal from a Course:** Students may withdraw from a course within the deadline (Last date for withdrawal is listed in the Academic Calendar — check timetable document). 
- For Sem 1 2025-26: Last date for withdrawal = October 20 (Monday).

**Course Substitution:** Students may substitute a registered course within the substitution deadline.
- For Sem 1 2025-26: Last date for substitution = August 27 (Wednesday).

**Add/Drop Period:** Typically in the first week of the semester. Specific dates are in the Academic Calendar.

**Backlog Courses:** If a student has failed or not cleared a course (NC/E in a CDC), they must re-register and clear it. This disrupts the normal course progression pattern and requires strategic planning.

# CHUNK 010 — PRACTICE SCHOOL (PS-1 AND PS-2)

Practice School is BITS's unique programme linking academics with industry. Students work at real companies in their field.

**PS-1 (Practice School I):**
- Duration: ~7-8 weeks (Summer — typically May/June to July)
- For: 2nd-year students (after completing 2-1 or 2-2)
- Application: Students apply in 2-2 semester
- Outcome: Live industry experience; typically non-credit but graded

**PS-2 (Practice School II):**
- Duration: ~5-6 months (one full semester)
- For: 4th-year single degree / 5th-year dual degree students
- Students go to the company instead of attending regular classes
- PS-2 is equivalent to a semester; graded with a Thesis
- CGPA up to 2 semesters before PS-2 is considered for company allocation
- PS-2 begins: October 6, 2025 (Sem 1 2025-26)
- PS-2 ends: December 21, 2025 (Sem 1 2025-26)

**Practice School Division** handles company engagement, listings, and student-company matching.

**Academic Timeline Overview:**
- 1-1 and 1-2: Only CDCs (common for all students)
- 2-1: First HuEL (single degree) or HuEL + possibly DEI (dual degree)
- 2-2: Apply for PS-1 and Minor. More electives.
- PS-1: Summer after 2nd year
- 3-1: SIs (Summer Internships for single degree). Hectic; fewer free credits for dual degree.
- Between 3-2 and 4-1: SI or Research Internship (RI) if eligible.
- 4-1: Placements; free credits for single degree. Dual: finish remaining requirements.
- 4-2: PS-2 for single degree.
- 5-1 and 5-2 (Dual): Double PS-2 if done with all 21 courses, or PS-2 + Thesis.

**Tips:**
- Save a trusted cab driver's number.
- Google Maps is reliable for navigation.
- Metro is cost-effective for city travel.
- Best to have cash handy as not all local vendors accept UPI.

# CHUNK 018 — STUDENTS' UNION (SU) OVERVIEW

The Students' Union (SU) is the democratically elected student government of BPHC. All regular students (excluding Ph.D., Higher Degree Collaboration, and M.Phil.) are **ipso facto** members of the Students' Union (called the General Body or GB).

The SU's preamble commits to protecting student rights, promoting academic awareness, cultural affluence, physical welfare, and general welfare, and upholding integrity, righteousness, and liberty.

**Annual membership fee:** As per the fee structure (₹450 per year as per 2023 batch fee estimate). Additional amounts can be proposed by the Council but must be ratified by at least ⅔ of the Union.

**Key SU Bodies:**
1. Students' Union Council (SUC) — Supreme governing body
2. Corroboration and Review Committee (CRC) — Financial oversight
3. Election Commission (EC) — Conducts all elections
4. Students' Mess Council (SMC) — Mess liaison
5. Technical Senate — Autonomous technical body
6. Cultural Senate — Cultural activities
7. Sports Senate — Sports activities
8. Departments (CoStAA, CoSSAc, StuCCA) — Fest organizers
9. Clubs — Cultural, technical, sports organizations

# CHUNK 019 — STUDENTS' UNION COUNCIL (SUC)

**Composition of the Council:**
- President
- General Secretary
- Cultural Secretary (Boys)
- Cultural Secretary (Girls)
- Sports Secretary (Boys)
- Sports Secretary (Girls)
- Hostel Representatives (one per hostel)
- One ex-officio member (nominated from outgoing Council; no voting rights)

**Meeting Rules:**
- Quorum: ≥ ¾ of elected members
- Each elected member gets one vote
- Tie-breaker: President has casting vote
- Any elected member can convene a meeting

**No student can hold two posts** in the Council or any of the Union's organs.

# CHUNK 020 — SUC ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

**(a) President:**
- Head of the Union and Council
- Signs authority for FT1 and FT3 financial transactions
- Can speak on behalf of Union with any authority (without prior consultation if urgent; must be ratified by ⅔ of Council within one week)
- May fix charges from Union members for activities (with Associate Dean SWD approval + ⅔ Council ratification)
- In President's absence: General Secretary performs duties

**(b) General Secretary:**
- Responsible for Council meetings: keeps minutes, publishes within 2 working days
- Maintains inventory of all Union property
- Convenes at least 2 GBMs per semester
- Convenes at least 4 Council meetings per semester
- In General Secretary's absence: President performs duties

**(c) Cultural Secretaries (Boys and Girls):**
- Organize intra-college cultural fests; preside over Cultural Senate
- May organize cultural events and exchange programmes
- Financial transactions: FT3 category

**(d) Sports Secretaries (Boys and Girls):**
- Organize intra-college sports fests; preside over Sports Senate
- Maintain inventory of sports equipment and facilities
- May recommend infrastructure/equipment procurement
- Financial transactions: FT3 category

**(e) Hostel Representatives:**
- Monitor inventory in their hostel; report discrepancies to Council and CRC
- Display souvenir orders and merchandise billing lists
- Call for at least one hostel meet per semester (minutes released within 2 working days)
- Advisory role to Cultural/Sports Secretaries for inter-Bhawan competitions (Renaissance/RIXA)

# CHUNK 021 — ELECTION COMMISSION (EC)

**Composition:** 6 members of the Union
**Faculty Advisor:** A faculty member chosen by the EC
**Ex-officio:** One member from vacating EC, in advisory capacity

**Powers:**
- Conduct free and fair elections for all SU posts
- Any complaint during elections must go to the EC
- Resolve disputes over interpretation of the Constitution (decision final and binding)
- Moderate General Body Meetings
- Audit CRC once a year in April
- Handle complaints against CRC members

**Restrictions:** EC members/ex-members are not eligible to contest/hold any post in the Council, SU organs, or any body whose elections are supervised by EC, at any point during their stay in college.

# CHUNK 022 — CORROBORATION AND REVIEW COMMITTEE (CRC)

**Composition:** 6 members of the Union
**Faculty Advisor:** Associate Dean, SWD
**Ex-officio:** One member from vacating CRC, in advisory capacity

**Powers:**
- Independent, self-sustaining body answerable to the Union
- Brings violations by Council or Union organs to notice of the Union
- Right to enquire any Council or organ member (bound to answer)
- Right to demand financial records (ledgers, invoices, credit memos, etc.) — failure to produce = flagrant violation
- Right to deputize CRC Representatives to check financial transactions
- Audited once a year in April by the EC
- Issues directives on financial transaction procedure (failure to comply = Constitution violation)
- Handles complaints against EC members

**Restrictions:** CRC members/ex-members are not eligible to contest/hold any post in Council or SU organs at any point during their stay in college.

# CHUNK 023 — FINANCIAL TRANSACTIONS (FT CATEGORIES)

**FT1:** Transactions of all bodies functioning during inter-college fests (ATMOS, Arena, Verba Maximus, Pearl). Responsible: President.

**FT2:** Transactions of all clubs (except Technical Senate clubs) for activities other than inter-college fests. Responsible: General Secretary.

**FT3:** Transactions of all Union organs (except Technical Senate) other than FT1 and FT2. Responsible: President.

**FT4:** Transactions of all bodies under the Technical Senate (other than FT1). Responsible: Technical Secretary.

**SUF (Student Union Fund) Allocation:**
- FT2 Transactions: 50% of SUF
- FT4 Transactions: 25% of SUF

**Budget Rules:**
- **Major propositions:** ATMOS, Arena, Verba Maximus, Pearl budgets. Must be presented to CRC at least 2 working days before Union presentation. Must be presented to Union at least 30 days before the fest. Must include 3 different provisional budgets (3 scenarios). Previous fest losses deducted from SU funds. Ratified by simple majority of Union.
- **Minor propositions:** Any other Council-organized activity. Presented to CRC, which can suggest changes or reject.

**Deals above ₹1,00,000:** A committee of President + General Secretary (± FOB member) must approve. Any MoU worth more than ₹1,00,000 requires all committee members' signatures.

# CHUNK 024 — GENERAL ELECTIONS

**Posts elected in General Elections:**
President, General Secretary, Cultural Secretaries (Boys + Girls), Sports Secretaries (Boys + Girls), Technical Secretary, Hostel Representatives, Higher Degree Representative, SMC members.

**Eligibility:**
- **President, General Secretary, Cultural Secretaries, Sports Secretaries:** Must be 3rd or 4th year student; on campus for both semesters of tenure; minimum CGPA 4.5 at time of contesting.
- **Technical Secretary:** Must be 3rd or 4th year; minimum CGPA 4.5; at least 1 year experience in Technical Senate; active member of Senate at time of application.
- **Hostel Representative:** Any year; elected by residents of that specific hostel.
- **SMC Members:** Any year except 1st year; must be on campus during both semesters.

**Election Procedure:**
- EC calls for nominations (elections not held within 1 week of nominations).
- No candidate may contest for more than one post.
- Elected student cannot hold any other post in any Union organ.
- Elected by single plurality (first-past-the-post) via secret ballot.
- Cultural/Sports Secretary (Boys) elected by male Union members only; (Girls) by female only.
- Hostel Reps elected by residents of their respective hostel.

**Oath:** Administered by EC at beginning of academic year. Council and SMC take oaths.

**Cessation of Office:**
- No-confidence motion passed
- Failure to meet eligibility criteria
- Found guilty by Disciplinary Committee or Examination Cell

# CHUNK 025 — REMOVAL FROM OFFICE (NO-CONFIDENCE MOTIONS)

**(A) President / General Secretary:**
- Initiation: ⅓ majority of concerned electorate OR ⅔ of entire elected Council submit signed memorandum to EC
- Valid if: ≥ ⅔ of concerned electorate votes
- Passed if: ≥ ⅔ of polled votes support motion

**(B) Hostel Reps / Cultural Secretaries / Sports Secretaries / Higher Degree Rep / SMC Members:**
- Initiation: ≥ ⅔ of entire Council OR ≥ ⅓ of concerned electorate
- Valid if: ≥ ⅔ of concerned electorate votes
- Passed if: ≥ ⅔ of polled votes support motion

**(C) EC / CRC Members:**
- Initiation: ≥ 80% of entire Council OR ⅓ of Union
- Signed memorandum to EC (for CRC) or CRC (for EC)
- Valid if: ≥ ⅔ of Union votes
- Passed if: ≥ ⅔ of polled votes support motion

# CHUNK 026 — FLAGRANT VIOLATIONS AND DISCIPLINARY ACTION

A **flagrant violation** results in the post-holder ceasing to hold their relevant post(s). A flagrant violation includes (but is not limited to) any act resulting in Disciplinary Committee or Examination Cell action.

**Reinstatement:** Motion of confidence must be passed by ≥ ⅔ of concerned electorate, initiated by ≥ ⅓ of concerned electorate, and submitted to EC within 3 days of removal from office. Requires prior permission of Associate Dean, SWD.

**Determination:** Whether an offense is a flagrant violation is decided by the EC in consultation with Associate Dean, SWD and EC's Faculty Advisor.

**CRC directives:** Failure to comply with CRC's directives on financial transaction procedure = Constitution violation.

**Procedure Manual violation:** Any violation of the Procedure Manual is considered a direct violation of the Students' Union Constitution and grounds for disciplinary action. An OT member convicted of violations can no longer be a member of any OT. Financial violations can result in profits from that activity being credited to the SUA.

# CHUNK 027 — GENERAL BODY MEETINGS (GBMs)

- Moderated by the EC (CRC may assist)
- Any Union member may propose a GBM; agenda must be ratified by EC
- EC releases minutes within one week of the GBM
- Union must be given at least 24 hours notice (Emergency GBM with less notice requires Associate Dean, SWD approval)

**GBM Categories:**
- **Major:** Constitution amendments, fest budgets, motion of confidence/no-confidence, referendums, other EC-deemed major agendas. Quorum: ≥ 10% of concerned electorate.
- **Minor:** Matters concerning particular departments, clubs, or hostel issues.

# CHUNK 028 — REFERENDUMS AND CONSTITUTIONAL AMENDMENTS

**Referendum:**
- Any major issue, or any proposed deduction from student accounts, must go to a referendum
- Initiated by ≥ ⅓ of Union OR ≥ ⅔ of Council
- Signed memorandum submitted to EC
- Valid only if EC deems it constitutionally valid
- Passed if ≥ ⅔ of polled votes support the motion

**Constitutional Amendments:**
- Initiated by ≥ ⅔ of Council OR ≥ ⅓ of Union
- Valid if ≥ ⅔ majority among Union members who vote
- Passed if ≥ ⅔ of general body members voting support it
- EC reviews Constitution every 3 years; reviewed constitution must be ratified by ⅔ of Union

**Adjuncts to Constitution:**
- Procedure Manual, Fest Charters, and Club Constitutions are all adjuncts
- In conflicts between Constitution and adjuncts, Constitution prevails

**Ordinances (Temporary Amendments):**
- Promulgated by EC in consultation with President and CRC
- Must be approved by Associate Dean, SWD and EC's Faculty Advisor
- Ceases to operate if not passed by Union with ⅔ majority within 2 weeks
- Cannot undermine President's authority, jeopardize liberty/wellbeing, or extend anyone's term

# CHUNK 033 — FESTS AT BPHC

**ATMOS (Assimilation of Technically Motivated Souls):**
- National annual technical fest. Usually held in October/November.
- Inter-college technology and management competitions, workshops, talks by industry leaders.
- Notable events: Robowars, Drone Racing.
- 3 days. Class work suspended during ATMOS (Sem 1 2025-26: November 5–7).
- Organized by: Technical Senate + Departments (CoStAA)

**ARENA:**
- National annual sports fest. Held in January.
- Inter-college sports competitions. Floodlit games, food stalls, vibrant atmosphere.
- 3 days + 3 more exciting days for Verba Maximus.
- Organized by: Sports Senate + Departments (CoSSAc)

**PEARL:**
- National annual cultural fest. Usually last official fest of academic year.
- Pro-shows, cultural performances, club events. Tickets sell out fast.
- Club working members get intime extension pass (stay outside hostel past 2 AM).
- Cultural club members perform on opening night in the auditorium.
- Organized by: Cultural Senate + Departments (StuCCA)

**Verba Maximus (VM):**
- National annual literary fest (unofficial name). Organized by ELAS and Journal Club.
- Speaking and writing contests. Food trucks, fun atmosphere. No class holidays.

**Launchpad:**
- Annual entrepreneurship summit by E-Cell BPHC.
- Internship opportunities, events, workshops, startup pitches, Shark Tank-inspired "Pitcher's Pilot."

**Sanskriti:**
- Cultural night organized by Regional Associations.
- RIXA (inter-Bhawan sports) and Renaissance (inter-Bhawan cultural competitions) happen during Sanskriti week.

**Fest Deductions:**
- GBM held before each fest to vote on per-student deduction options (e.g., ₹800/₹400/₹0).
- Higher deduction = larger-scale fest.
- Vote by show of hands; majority wins.
- Pro-shows include stand-up comedians, musicians — tickets via SWD account.

# CHUNK 041 — PROCEDURE MANUAL: MERCHANDISE AND ACTIVITIES

**Merchandise Rules:**
- Merchandise = any sale of goods collecting funds from general body members. No distinction between goods and services.
- Requires approval from SUC, CRC, and Associate Dean, SWD.
- GB members informed of prices at least 3 days before sale.
- Issued only via SWD deductions (Smart Campus).
- Organizations may sell only 2 items as merchandise per allotted slot.
- Only OT-1, OT-4, and OT-5 may issue GB merchandise.
- Profit cap: 20% on sale to GB. Can exceed only under extraordinary circumstances with Associate Dean SWD approval.
- Quality check on arrival of consignment (CRC must be present).

**Activity and Funding Rules:**
- No student shall conduct any campus activity for personal income.
- Raising funds from GB requires permission from SUC and Associate Dean SWD (except OT-5 which needs Technical Secretary and Associate Dean SWD approval). CRC must be informed in all cases.
- OT-3 can conduct events on campus (needing prior SUC + SWD approval). Price list declared only after SU approval; CRC informed in advance.
- All financial transactions for OT-4 and OT-5 need approval from CONVENER-IIC and Associate Dean SWD respectively.

**Inventory:**
- Includes raw materials, work in progress, and finished goods (current assets) of any club/department.
- All In-Kind Sponsorships retrieved by OTs or SUC are part of Inventory.
- SWD maintains a Vendors' Blacklist (updated end of every semester).
- Organizations must submit inventory reports at end of tenure (verified by CRC and sent to SWD).

# CHUNK 047 — KEY RULES AND POLICIES SUMMARY

**Academic:**
- Min 9, max 25 credits per semester
- Min CGPA 4.5 (First Degree); max 1 E grade per semester
- CDCs cannot be dropped; must pass to graduate
- Min 4 DEIs (12 credits) + 3 HuELs (8 credits) + 5 OpELs (15 credits) to graduate
- Withdrawal deadline: typically ~12 weeks into semester
- Comprehensive exams: FN (9:30–12:30) or AN (2:00–5:00)

**Hostel:**
- In-time: 2:00 AM (generally)
- Rooms have LAN port, whiteboard, almirah, bed, desk, chair
- Hot water from tap (not shower)

**SU Constitutional Thresholds:**
- Simple majority: 50%+1 votes
- Two-thirds (⅔): ~67% — for most major decisions
- One-third (⅓): ~33% — for initiating motions
- 80%: Required to initiate no-confidence against EC/CRC member from Council side

**Financial:**
- Deals > ₹1,00,000 need President + General Secretary committee approval
- Advance Requisition Forms taken up by CRC within 1 week of submission
- Bills must be submitted to CRC and Technical Secretary within 7 days of expenditure (for Tech Senate funds)
- Minor programme fee: ₹28,000 (two installments)
